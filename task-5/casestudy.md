## 📄 Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, targeting users who rely on digital payment apps for everyday transactions. This type of fraud involves tricking users into authorizing payments or revealing sensitive details such as UPI PINs or OTPs.

A typical UPI fraud occurs in several steps. First, the scammer contacts the victim via phone, SMS, or social media, often posing as a bank official, customer support agent, or buyer/seller. Next, they create urgency or trust by claiming a refund, prize, or payment issue. The victim is then asked to click on a malicious link or scan a QR code. In many cases, the scammer sends a “collect request” and convinces the victim that entering their UPI PIN is required to receive money. Once the victim enters the PIN, the money is instead transferred to the scammer’s account.

College students and young adults are frequent targets due to their high use of apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm, and sometimes limited awareness of fraud tactics. The consequences can include financial loss, compromised personal data, and emotional stress.

This case highlights the importance of awareness and caution. Users must remember that receiving money does not require entering a PIN and should always verify requests before completing any transaction.
