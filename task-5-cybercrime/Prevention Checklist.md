## Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
Use strong, unique passwords for each account and enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.<br>
Never click on suspicious links in emails, SMS, or WhatsApp messages—especially those asking for login or payment details.<br>
Keep your devices updated with the latest security patches and antivirus software.<br>
Avoid using public Wi-Fi for banking or sensitive transactions; use a VPN if necessary.<br>
Do not share OTPs, PINs, or passwords with anyone—even if they claim to be from your bank or a trusted service.<br>
Verify job offers, internships, and scholarship links before applying—watch out for fake recruitment scams targeting students.<br>
Be cautious while sharing personal information on social media; adjust privacy settings to limit exposure.<br>
Regularly monitor your bank statements and transaction history for any unauthorized activity.<br>
For UPI safety: Double-check the UPI ID or QR code before making payments; scammers often use similar-looking IDs.<br>
For UPI safety: Remember that receiving money does NOT require entering a PIN—only payments do.<br>
